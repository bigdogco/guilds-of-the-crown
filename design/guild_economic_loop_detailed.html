Guild — Detailed Economic Diagrams

Expanded design decisions for each phase of the economic loop

Production

Resource gathering — sources

Resources enter the business from two distinct origins: owned extraction buildings or open natural gathering spots in the world.

Production

Resource gathering — output paths

Extraction building output flows to the market automatically, or the player can buy directly from the building. Natural spots flow straight to the workshop.

Trade

City AI emergency import

If a resource stays critically low on the market for too long, the city AI triggers an emergency import from other towns — maintaining a stock floor.

Production

Hiring employees

NPCs are hired from a finite town pool. Skill grows through work, triggering wage rise requests. No grudges — NPCs rehire to anyone offering acceptable wages.

Production

Crafting system

Fixed recipes per trade. Crafting always succeeds if ingredients are present. Worker skill reduces time. Small random chance of bonus output.

Trade

City market & selling

All businesses deposit into the shared city market pool. Price is always automatic — supply and demand driven. NPCs, players, and the city AI all buy from the same pool.

Trade

City AI procurement

The city buys from the market on a schedule to upkeep services — guards need swords and armour, administration needs paper. Scales with city size. Deferred demand accumulates and spikes price when stock returns.

Finances

Finances flow

Revenue flows in real time on each sale. Wages, upkeep, and taxes are deducted at end of day. A city loan provides a grace period lifeline before bankruptcy.

Growth

Reinvestment & growth

Profit can be reinvested into buildings, upgrades, staff, a second trade, or extraction buildings. All construction takes in-game time. Growth feeds back into better production capacity.
